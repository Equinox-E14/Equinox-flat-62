A note from the vendor

Twenty years.
One remarkable home.

I have often thought that this home gave me something increasingly rare in London: room to breathe.

Over twenty years, the river, the open sky, and the changing light around Island Gardens gradually shaped the rhythm of daily life here. Morning coffee outside, walks beside the Thames, the ease of stepping into Greenwich whenever the mood suited — these became rituals, not activities.

This became far more than simply a home. It became my sanctuary within the city. The true luxury here isn't something you can measure in square footage. It is the daily feeling of peace it provides.

I vividly remember the day I first saw the terrace. It was one of those glorious, sunny and warm April days we sometimes get. I was exiting Island Gardens DLR station and, on a complete whim, I walked into the marketing office of the development. I asked about viewing the flat, and the sales agent took me straight up to see it. I walked into the living room that was flooded with light and straight out onto the terrace. I knew at that moment I was lost. I signed the purchase contract a couple of days later.

— The vendor · Island Gardens · E14 · Twenty years

The terrace is where this home quietly expands — a second living room that happens to sit under open sky.

← City of London Canary Wharf ↑ Cutty Sark ↓

Facing west, the terrace catches the afternoon sun from early spring through to late autumn. Thirteen metres of it. The City of London sits straight ahead. Lean north on the balustrade and you see Canary Wharf. Look south and the Cutty Sark's mast breaks the Greenwich roofline.

Breakfast outside became habitual. Meals stretched into the evening. A quick drink after work turned into an hour as the light shifted across the skyline. Planting softens the edges, the outdoor kitchen makes it genuinely practical, and the water feature adds a quiet rhythm.

As dusk settles, the light doesn't disappear so much as change character — windows start to glow, the skyline gradually illuminates, and the terrace becomes a calm vantage point over a softly lit London at night.

The terrace isn't a bonus feature. It's the reason the home works the way it does.
