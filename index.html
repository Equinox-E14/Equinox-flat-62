A note from the vendor

Twenty years.
One remarkable home.

I have often thought that this home gave me something increasingly rare in London: room to breathe.

Over twenty years, the river, the open sky, and the changing light around Island Gardens gradually shaped the rhythm of daily life here. Morning coffee outside, walks beside the Thames, and the ease of stepping into Greenwich whenever the mood suited became rituals, not just activities.

This became far more than simply a flat; it has been my sanctuary within the city. The true luxury here isn't something you can easily measure in a standard estate agent layout. It is the daily feeling of total peace it provides.

Leaving for the south coast now feels like the right next chapter, but the quiet rhythm of this place will be missed enormously.

A peninsula wrapped in
green and water.

The Isle of Dogs is bounded by water on three sides. That unique geography gives it something rare for inner London: genuine open breathing space, a working city farm, and miles of uninterrupted river path right on your doorstep.

Island Gardens Adjacent

Mudchute Park & Farm 10 min walk
32 acres of forested nature reserve and open countryside, home to one of Europe's largest city farms. Watching horses graze with Canary Wharf rising behind them never gets old.

The Thames Path National Trail runs the entire north bank of the Isle of Dogs loop — running past historic launch sites and the Docklands Sailing Centre before sweeping into Canary Wharf. The full loop is approximately 7 km, completely flat, and beautifully traffic-free.

A sense of place

The Island beneath
the glass.

Before the clean modern lines of Equinox, this exact architectural plot served as a core connection point for London’s historic maritime and transit systems.

In 1872, this exact plot was the terminus of the Millwall Extension Railway, where passengers stepped off steam trains directly onto a river ferry jetty. In 1999, when the modern DLR line transitioned completely beneath the Thames to Greenwich, Equinox was built directly atop the footprint of that historic gateway.

1860s

Brunel's SS Great Eastern launched from the adjacent Millwall waterfront.

1999
